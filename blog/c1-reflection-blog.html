What's your take the DBC/EDA experience?

-Their aim is to keep the course on the cutting edge of industry standards.
-The phase system run's on a 3 weeks cycle. Student's are reassessed at the end of these phase cycle's.
-The focus is more on the essential concepts and principles of the skills being studies. Mastery will be an ongoing process.
-Pair programming is used to teach the essential interpersonal skills required to both pass the DBC and function in the workplace.

What are your impressions?

EDA might not be as structured and organized as I had hope. There may be some inherent risks to a course style like this, as the course lessons and material are perpetually in a state of 'work-in-progress'. However this style of 'continuous integrations' is probably the best solution to the rapidly developing tech industry. We will need to take a greater degree of responsibility for our learning in this type of environment and cannot assume that everything we need to know is taught in this course. Getting hired will be a repeating feed-back loop of; rejection, improvement, resubmission. It's best to get used to that mindset now.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I see it giving me the opportunity to develop my interpersonal skills in parallel with technical skills.

Have your expectations of EDA Changed? If so, how?

Not really. The video; Shereef's Fireside Chat, is 3 years old and gave me mostly a perspective on the dev boot camp experience. Perhaps I need to learn more about the EDA 'experience' to compare and contrast the two better.
